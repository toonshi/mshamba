# Mshamba Pilot Programme

---

## Overview

**Goal:** Complete one full cycle — farmer raises capital, deploys it, generates profit, investors get paid.

**Timeline:** 15 months total
**Target raise:** KES 500,000–1,000,000 (start small, prove the model)
**Target investors:** 10–20 people you know personally

Keep it manual. No smart contracts for the pilot. Spreadsheets, M-Pesa, and a lawyer. Technology comes after proof.

---

## Phase 0: Find the Farmer
**Weeks 1–4**

The only goal this month is a signed letter of intent from one farmer.

**Search checklist:**
- [ ] List every family member or friend connected to farming
- [ ] Contact 3 university agriculture departments (UoN, Egerton, JKUAT)
- [ ] Visit one county agricultural office (Kiambu, Nakuru, or Muranga are closest)
- [ ] Call 2 cooperative societies and ask to speak to their secretary
- [ ] Ask your co-founder to do the same independently — double the surface area

**Farmer qualification checklist (must pass all):**
- [ ] Farming actively for minimum 3 years
- [ ] Has at least one documented revenue source (school supply, market, cooperative offtake)
- [ ] Has a specific, costed use for new capital (not vague — "I need KES 300K for a drip irrigation system from XYZ supplier")
- [ ] Willing to open their records to you
- [ ] Willing to have 10–20 strangers as minority co-owners
- [ ] Has an M-Pesa registered account
- [ ] You would personally invest KES 10,000 in this farm

**Red flags — walk away if:**
- [ ] No records at all, not even informal ones
- [ ] The capital need is vague or keeps changing
- [ ] They want the money before any agreement is signed
- [ ] They're uncomfortable with oversight or reporting

**Deliverable:** One farmer who has verbally agreed to proceed.

## Phase 1: Baseline Audit
**Weeks 5–10**

You need clean data before you can sell this to investors. This phase is about building the truth document for the farm.

**Records collection checklist:**
- [ ] 3 years of sales records (receipts, M-Pesa statements, cooperative payment slips)
- [ ] 3 years of input costs (seeds, fertilizer, labour — even rough logs)
- [ ] Current farm photos (wide shots, close-ups of crops, infrastructure)
- [ ] GPS coordinates of the farm boundary
- [ ] Title deed or proof of land tenure (leasehold acceptable)
- [ ] Any existing contracts with buyers (schools, hotels, cooperatives)
- [ ] List of all equipment owned with approximate age and condition
- [ ] Water source documentation (borehole permit, river proximity, rain-fed)

**Farm visit checklist (go there in person):**
- [ ] Walk the entire farm boundary
- [ ] Photograph every piece of infrastructure
- [ ] Meet the farm workers if any
- [ ] Verify the land size matches what the farmer claims
- [ ] Confirm the crop being grown matches the sales records
- [ ] Ask to see the buyer — call the school or hotel yourself if possible

**Financial summary checklist:**
- [ ] Calculate average annual revenue (last 3 years)
- [ ] Calculate average annual costs (last 3 years)
- [ ] Calculate average net profit (last 3 years)
- [ ] Identify the best and worst year and understand why
- [ ] Document the specific investment being proposed and its expected impact

**Deliverable:** A 2–3 page farm brief. Plain English. Real numbers. Photos attached.

---

## Phase 2: Deal Structuring
**Weeks 11–14**

Before approaching investors, the deal terms must be locked and legally documented.

**Valuation checklist:**
- [ ] Calculate farm value using income approach (net profit × 5–7x multiplier)
- [ ] Sanity check against land market value in that county
- [ ] Determine capital needed (specific amount, not rounded up)
- [ ] Determine equity percentage being sold (target: 20–25%)
- [ ] Calculate price per share (divide equity by number of shares issued)
- [ ] Set minimum investment amount (suggest KES 10,000)
- [ ] Set maximum investment per person (suggest KES 100,000 for the pilot)

**Legal checklist (hire a lawyer — budget KES 50,000–80,000):**
- [ ] Draft a simple shareholder agreement between farmer and investors
- [ ] Include dividend distribution clause (% of profit, when paid, how calculated)
- [ ] Include milestone-based fund release schedule
- [ ] Include reporting obligations for the farmer (quarterly updates minimum)
- [ ] Include what happens if the farm fails (limited liability — investors lose their stake, not more)
- [ ] Include exit clause (can investors sell their shares and to whom)
- [ ] Farmer signs the agreement before any money moves

**Escrow checklist:**
- [ ] Open a dedicated M-Pesa Paybill or bank account for this farm only
- [ ] Both you and the farmer must be signatories
- [ ] Define the milestone release schedule in writing:
  - [ ] Milestone 1: [specific deliverable] → releases KES [X]
  - [ ] Milestone 2: [specific deliverable] → releases KES [X]
  - [ ] Milestone 3: [specific deliverable] → releases KES [X]
- [ ] Define what counts as proof for each milestone (invoice, photo, receipt)

**Deliverable:** Signed shareholder agreement. Escrow account open. Milestone schedule documented.

---

## Phase 3: Investor Recruitment
**Weeks 15–18**

Your first investors will not come from a marketing campaign. They will come from your phone contacts.

**Investor target checklist:**
- [ ] List 50 people in your network who have KES 10,000–100,000 to spare
- [ ] Prioritise: diaspora family, employed professionals, business owners
- [ ] Do not approach anyone who cannot afford to lose the investment

**Pitch materials checklist:**
- [ ] 1-page farm summary (the brief from Phase 1, simplified further)
- [ ] Clear statement of terms: how much, what % equity, when dividends are paid
- [ ] Photos of the farm
- [ ] The farmer's name, face, and story — investors are backing a person
- [ ] Your own personal investment in the farm (you must invest your own money)
- [ ] Contact details and how to send money

**Investor onboarding checklist (for each investor):**
- [ ] Verbal explanation of the deal and the risks
- [ ] They confirm they understand this is equity, not a loan — they can lose it
- [ ] They sign the shareholder agreement
- [ ] They send their investment to the escrow Paybill
- [ ] You send them an M-Pesa confirmation and a signed share certificate (even a PDF is fine)
- [ ] Add them to a WhatsApp group for this farm

**IFO close checklist:**
- [ ] Target amount reached OR deadline passed (give it 30 days maximum)
- [ ] If target not reached — decide: proceed with partial raise or refund everyone
- [ ] Send all investors a confirmation of their shareholding percentage
- [ ] Announce the close on the WhatsApp group

**Deliverable:** Target capital raised. All investors have signed agreements and share certificates.

---

## Phase 4: Capital Deployment & Monitoring
**Months 5–14**

This is the longest phase. Your job is to make sure the money goes where it was supposed to and the farm actually grows.

**Month 5 — First milestone release:**
- [ ] Farmer submits proof for Milestone 1 (invoice, photo, receipt)
- [ ] You verify it in person or via video call
- [ ] Release funds from escrow to farmer's M-Pesa
- [ ] Document the release with a screenshot and share with the WhatsApp group

**Every month — monitoring checklist:**
- [ ] Receive monthly update from farmer (voice note is fine if they're not literate)
- [ ] Record current crop condition (photos)
- [ ] Record any issues — weather, pests, market price changes
- [ ] Post a brief update to the investor WhatsApp group
- [ ] Update your tracking spreadsheet

**Tracking spreadsheet (build this in Google Sheets):**
- [ ] Column: Month
- [ ] Column: Revenue this month
- [ ] Column: Costs this month
- [ ] Column: Net profit this month
- [ ] Column: Cumulative profit
- [ ] Column: Milestone status
- [ ] Column: Funds released to date
- [ ] Column: Notes

**Every milestone — release checklist:**
- [ ] Farmer submits documented proof
- [ ] You verify (in person for large amounts)
- [ ] Both signatories approve the release
- [ ] Funds sent via M-Pesa
- [ ] WhatsApp group updated
- [ ] Spreadsheet updated

**Deliverable:** All milestones hit. Farm operating with new capital deployed. Monthly investor updates sent consistently.

---

## Phase 5: Harvest & First Distribution
**Month 15**

This is the moment everything was built for. An investor receiving money on their phone from a farm they co-own.

**Harvest documentation checklist:**
- [ ] Record total yield (kg, bags, litres — whatever the crop measures in)
- [ ] Record total revenue from sales (M-Pesa statements, receipts)
- [ ] Record total costs for the season
- [ ] Calculate net profit
- [ ] Photograph the harvest
- [ ] Get a written or recorded statement from the farmer

**Dividend calculation checklist:**
- [ ] Confirm total net profit figure
- [ ] Confirm each investor's shareholding percentage from the agreement
- [ ] Calculate each investor's dividend (their % × net profit)
- [ ] Deduct Mshamba's 5% platform share
- [ ] Prepare a distribution table in your spreadsheet

**Distribution checklist:**
- [ ] Send each investor their dividend via M-Pesa
- [ ] Screenshot every transaction
- [ ] Send each investor a summary: amount invested, dividend received, return %
- [ ] Post the full distribution summary to the WhatsApp group
- [ ] Thank the farmer publicly in the group

**Deliverable:** Every investor paid. Every transaction documented. The cycle is complete.

---

## Phase 6: Document Everything
**Week after distribution**

This is what you take to your next investor — the person who funds your Series A or your next 10 farms.

**Case study checklist:**
- [ ] Starting farm financials vs ending farm financials
- [ ] Capital raised, from how many investors, in how many days
- [ ] Milestones hit (on time or delayed, and why)
- [ ] Final investor return (annualised %)
- [ ] Farmer's net cash flow vs equivalent loan scenario
- [ ] What broke and how you fixed it
- [ ] Quotes from the farmer and 2–3 investors
- [ ] Photos: before, during, harvest, distribution

**Regulatory checklist (start this during Phase 4, not after):**
- [ ] Contact CMA and ask about the regulatory sandbox
- [ ] Document all investor interactions as evidence of compliance intent
- [ ] Get a legal opinion on whether your pilot structure requires licensing
- [ ] Begin preparing your sandbox application using the pilot data

---

## The One Metric That Matters

At the end of Month 15, you need one number:

**Did the investor get their money back plus a return?**

If yes — you have a product. Fund the next 10 farms.
If no — you have a lesson. Find out exactly why and fix that one thing.
